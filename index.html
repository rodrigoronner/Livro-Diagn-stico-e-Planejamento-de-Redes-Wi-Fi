Channel Planning
2,4 GHz 2412–2472 MHz · 13 channels · 22 MHz width
Non-overlapping channel (1, 6, 11)
Overlapping channel — avoid
Mandatory rule: use only channels 1, 6, and 11 in 2.4 GHz. Any other channel causes Adjacent Channel Interference (ACI), which CSMA/CA does not detect and which is worse than co-channel interference. Always use 20 MHz width in this band. Ch. 9 Section 9.3.
5 GHz Over 20 non-overlapping channels · UNII-1 to UNII-3
UNII-1 (no DFS)
UNII-2A/2C (DFS required)
UNII-3 (no DFS)
DFS: the AP automatically releases the channel upon detecting a radar signal. In environments near airports, coastlines, or military bases, prefer UNII-1 (36–48) or UNII-3 (149–165). In urban environments without these sources, DFS channels are generally safe and offer extra spectrum.
Recommended width: residential → 80 MHz · dense corporate → 40 MHz · high density → 20 MHz. Ch. 9 Section 9.3 / Ch. 10 Section 10.2.
6 GHz Wi-Fi 6E / Wi-Fi 7 · Approved by Anatel 2021 (Resolution 754)
59 20 MHz channelsavailable in the 5925–7125 MHz band
7 160 MHz channelsnon-overlapping — ideal for Wi-Fi 6E
Wi-Fi 7320 MHz channels — being deployed in the market
Advantages: no legacy devices · no DFS · no accumulated interference · 160 MHz viable in most scenarios
Use 6 GHz for modern clients and keep 5 GHz for backward compatibility. Ch. 10 Section 10.2.
